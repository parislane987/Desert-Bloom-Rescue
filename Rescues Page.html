Discover Our Newest Rescues

Ziggy the Macaw

Ziggy was rescued from a cramped apartment where he suffered abuse and lived without proper attention, love, or stimulation. He’s currently thriving here at Desert Bloom in a large aviary and loves to show off his bright and boisterous personality!

Kay the Sugar Glider

Kay, along with dozens of other gliders, was rescued from an unlicensed breeder who kept the animals in terrible condition and did not properly care for them. She’s now safe and thriving in her natural habitat with our loving staff. 

Benny the Serval

Benny was saved from an illegal exotic pet trade operation. He was shy at first, but he’s now adapted to his spacious outdoor enclosure and spends his days leaping and lounging happily.

Juno the Ball Python

Juno was found in an abandoned storage unit, malnourished and dehydrated. After months of care and rehab, he is now healthy and happy here at Desert Bloom. 

Nora the Capuchin Monkey

Nora was surrendered by an owner who realized she might be more of an undertaking than they realized. Thanks to this responsible choice, Nora now lives happily in a natural habitat where she enjoys climbing, swinging, and playing with her favorite toys.

Fran the Uromastyx

Fran was found in a neglect case involving multiple reptiles left without heat or food. He’s now living happily and comfortably in his enclosure with the expert care of our staff.
